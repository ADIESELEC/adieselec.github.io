Certificate of Compliance

Certificate of Compliance (COC) — What It Covers

A Certificate of Compliance (COC) confirms your electrical installation meets South African safety standards. It's a legal requirement, not a nice-to-have.

  • →Required to sell a property. No valid COC, no transfer — it's one of the first documents a conveyancer will ask for.
  • →Often required by insurers. A claim involving electrical damage or fire can be affected without a valid, up-to-date certificate.
  • →Covers the full installation. Wiring, earthing, DB board, and any additions made since the last certificate was issued.
  • →Valid for as long as no changes are made. Any new circuit or addition means the certificate needs to be updated.
